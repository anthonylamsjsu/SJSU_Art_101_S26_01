Time moves in circles...

yet we perceive it as a line.

Nature reminds us

that change is not an ending

but a return.

scroll to begin

I. Spring

Something stirs beneath the soil.

A promise kept in cold darkness

breaks into light.

Petals unfurl like shy secrets.

The world exhales—

and begins again.

II. Summer

Light pours down like honey.

Everything leans toward the sun—

aching to be full.

Time stretches, golden and slow.

We are alive,

and we are burning.

III. Autumn

The world softens into amber.

Leaves fall like letters

written in a forgotten language.

Decay is not defeat.

It is a quiet release—

letting go to make room.

IV. Winter

The world holds its breath.

White covers everything—

a blank page waiting.

In the stillness, we remember

that beneath the ice

the seed still dreams.

And so it turns.

Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter.

Endless. Patient. True.
