Using Discord data

This project uses a dataset inspired by Discord. You can request your own copy of your Discord data and use it to replace the sample data in this site.

How to get your Discord data:

  1. Open Discord (desktop or browser).
  2. Click the gear icon (User Settings) at the bottom left.
  3. Go to Privacy & Safety in the left sidebar.
  4. Scroll to Data Request and click Request a copy of your data.
  5. Discord will email you when your data is ready (often within a few days). Download the ZIP and extract it.

The export includes account info, messages (DMs and servers), and activity. You can open the JSON/CSV files, summarize message counts by month or server, and update data/discord-data.js with your real numbers and word counts so this portrait reflects your actual usage.

Narrative

This data self portrait turns my Discord activity into a simple narrative: when I message (activity by month and day of week), where I hang out (servers), and how I talk (word frequency). Right now the site uses sample data so it runs without waiting for an export. Once I have my real Discord package, I’ll replace the numbers in discord-data.js to make the visualizations and story about me.
