Exploring Materials

After the assignment introduction, Professor Martinez took us on a tour of the materials room. It was like an artist's candy store—rows of oil paints in every color imaginable, tubes of acrylics, watercolors, pastels, charcoal, and so much more.

"Don't be afraid to experiment," she told us. "Some of the best art comes from trying something new, from making mistakes and learning from them." I picked up a tube of ultramarine blue paint, feeling its weight in my hand, and imagined the colors I could create.

I also discovered materials I'd never worked with before—gouache, ink, and various types of paper and canvas. The possibilities were overwhelming in the best way. I grabbed a sketchbook and started making notes about techniques I wanted to try.
