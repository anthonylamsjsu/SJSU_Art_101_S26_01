Creating My First Artwork

Back at my easel, I set up a canvas and began sketching. The pencil felt natural in my hand as I drew the outline of the SJSU campus, with the art building at the center. I wanted to capture that feeling of arrival, of beginning something new.

I mixed my paints carefully—warm yellows for the morning sun, cool blues for the sky, and vibrant greens for the campus trees. As I worked, I lost track of time. The world outside the studio faded away, and it was just me, my canvas, and the creative flow.

Professor Martinez came by and watched me work for a moment. "Good use of color," she said. "Don't be afraid to push those values darker in the shadows." Her feedback was encouraging and constructive. I felt like I was already growing as an artist.
